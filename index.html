What is a CDN?

A Content Delivery Network (CDN) is a geographically distributed group of servers which work together to provide fast delivery of Internet content.

How CDNs Work

CDNs cache content at multiple locations around the world, reducing the distance between users and website resources.

Benefits of CDNs

  • Improved page load times
  • Reduced latency
  • Increased content availability
  • Enhanced security

Fun Facts About CDNs

Did you know? CDNs serve a large portion of the Internet content today, including web objects (text, graphics and scripts), downloadable objects (media files, software, documents), applications (e-commerce, portals), live streaming media, on-demand streaming media, and social networks.
